# PRD Quality Review — Blood Donor Availability Matcher

## Overall verdict

This is a solid PRD for what it is: a fast-drafted, terse-stakeholder internal tool with two mid-draft scope reversals, and it does the hard part well — the privacy reversal and the Twilio free-trial blocker are surfaced honestly via `[NOTE FOR PM]` callouts rather than smoothed over, and FRs mostly carry testable consequences rather than aspirational prose. It is let down by two mechanical breaks in the Assumptions Index roundtrip that undercut the document's own stated integrity mechanism (§0 promises every inference is "tagged inline `[ASSUMPTION]` and indexed in §9" — this isn't fully true), and by leaving the one abuse vector with real financial and PII-exposure consequences (unauthenticated Searchers triggering paid SMS/email + PII reveal at will) as an open question rather than a forced decision. There is also no NFR treatment of the PII/privacy risk the document itself calls its central tension. Verdict: **Good** — decision-ready with fixable gaps, not launch-ready as written.

## Decision-readiness — adequate

The privacy reversal (broadcast-only → direct donor-list reveal) is the PRD's central tension, and it is genuinely surfaced, not buried: §1 Vision names it explicitly as "a deliberate trade toward speed and directness over the anonymized, broadcast-only model considered earlier," cross-referenced to a `[NOTE FOR PM]` in Constraints & Guardrails › Privacy that cites a concrete stat (67% donor concern about contact-info misuse) rather than hand-waving. The Twilio free-trial blocker is called out with unusual directness — "this is a functional blocker for real-world use of FR-8's SMS channel, not just a cost concern" (Constraints & Guardrails › Cost) — exactly the kind of thing that gets softened into a footnote in weaker PRDs. That said:
- **[High]** Searcher-triggered abuse/cost vector left as an open question, not a decision (§6.2, §8 Open Question 4, §4.6) — every unauthenticated search fires a real, paid Twilio SMS + SendGrid email to every Match and reveals a Donor's PII, and the only defense (per-IP rate limiting) has thresholds explicitly marked TBD (Open Question 3). Given the stakeholder was terse and unlikely to revisit, shipping this as an open question rather than a forced choice (e.g. "Searcher must OTP-verify before first search" or "cap N notifications/IP/day") risks the PM never coming back to it before it becomes a live cost/privacy incident. *Fix:* Convert OQ4 into an explicit MVP decision — even "accepted risk, revisit post-launch" is better than silent deferral, and set a concrete rate-limit default now rather than leaving OQ3 fully open.
- **[Low]** The working title is still unresolved ("*Working title — confirm.*", §0/title) — trivial, but loose ends like this shouldn't survive to a document meant to seed architecture/epics.

## Substance over theater — thin

The four UJs are earned, not boilerplate: they carry specific, non-generic detail (Amara's father needing O-negative at a specific hospital in Gulberg; Rohan's SMS text reproduced verbatim; Priya's registration-then-later-deletion arc reusing the same persona across UJ-3/UJ-4, which is good practice rather than a fresh throwaway name each time). FRs carry real "Consequences (testable)" sections instead of restating the requirement in other words.
- **[Medium]** NFR coverage is thin relative to how much weight the PRD puts on the privacy/PII tension. The only NFR-flavored content is SM-1/SM-2 (timing) and a single "Feature-specific NFRs" line under FR-8 about Twilio/SendGrid being external dependencies. Nothing addresses data-at-rest protection, transport security, log/retention of phone numbers, or access control for the OTP session — despite §1, Constraints & Guardrails › Privacy, and Open Question 6 all treating PII exposure as the document's central risk. *Fix:* Add a short NFR subsection (or fold into Constraints & Guardrails) translating Open Question 6 into at least placeholder obligations (e.g. "phone numbers stored encrypted at rest; OTP session tokens expire after use") so architecture isn't left to invent the PII-handling bar from scratch.

## Strategic coherence — strong

§1 Vision has an actual thesis — "replaces that scramble with a structured, always-current registry" plus the explicit speed-over-privacy trade — and the rest of the document reads as consequences of that thesis (predefined Area over geolocation, direct reveal over broadcast, dual-channel notification as a hedge against either side missing the other) rather than an unordered capability list. The addendum's Decision History (v0→v1→v2) reinforces that the current shape is a considered endpoint, not an oversight, which is exactly what a document surviving a terse, reversal-prone stakeholder needs.

## Done-ness clarity — strong

Nearly every FR has a "Consequences (testable)" block an engineer could build against: FR-3's day-90 boundary condition, FR-4's "never shown as fallback" exclusion, FR-9's "access only to the registration matching that verified phone number" isolation guarantee. Vague terms ("clear inline error," "clear, worded empty state," "clear rate-limit response") appear but are each anchored to a concrete consequence bullet or a cross-referenced Open Question about the exact threshold, so they read as "left to architecture" rather than "left vague forever."
- **[Low]** FR-1's "Registration is rejected with a clear inline error if blood type, phone, or at least one Area is missing" doesn't specify phone-number format validation (e.g. Pakistani mobile format) even though every downstream feature (OTP, SMS notification) depends on the number being dialable — minor, but worth a line since a malformed number silently breaks FR-2 and FR-8 rather than failing at intake. *Fix:* Add a consequence bullet to FR-1 requiring phone-format validation at submission.

## Scope honesty — thin

Non-Goals (§5) are genuinely used to close off real ambiguity (GPS/Haversine, WhatsApp, hospital integration, other cities) rather than restating obvious exclusions, and `[NOTE FOR PM]` tags land at real tensions (§1, §4.6, §6.2, Constraints & Guardrails › Safety and Cost). But the Assumptions Index roundtrip promised in §0 is broken in two places:
- **[High]** Assumptions Index roundtrip broken (§9 vs. "Integration and Dependencies" section) — the inline tag `[ASSUMPTION: OTP delivery channel — SMS vs. another method — not respecified after the WhatsApp-to-Twilio/SendGrid switch; assumed Twilio SMS is reused for OTP too.]` under "Integration and Dependencies" has **no corresponding entry in §9**, even though §9 claims to index every inline assumption. *Fix:* Add a §9 bullet: "Integration and Dependencies — OTP delivery channel assumed to be Twilio SMS (not respecified after WhatsApp was dropped)."
- **[Medium]** Conversely, §9's last bullet ("Constraints & Guardrails (Cost) — Twilio free trial's recipient-verification limitation and SendGrid free-tier volume cap are treated as MVP-acceptable...") indexes content that is tagged `[NOTE FOR PM]` in the body, not `[ASSUMPTION]` — there is no matching inline `[ASSUMPTION]` tag for this entry to point to. This mixes two different callout mechanisms (inferred-fact tags vs. PM-attention tags) under one index that claims to track only the former. *Fix:* Either add an inline `[ASSUMPTION]` tag at that Cost bullet, or move this entry out of §9 and into a parallel "NOTE FOR PM index" if one is warranted.

## Downstream usability — adequate

Glossary terms (Donor, Searcher, Blood Type, Area, Eligibility Window, Match, Search, Notification, OTP) are used consistently and capitalized consistently throughout §4 and §7 — no drift spotted. SM-to-FR validation links (SM-1→FR-1/FR-2, SM-2→FR-5/FR-6/FR-7, SM-3→FR-6/FR-7, SM-4→FR-8) are complete and correctly resolve. Cross-references (§4.3, §4.6, §9, Open Question N) all resolve to real sections.
- **[Medium]** "Realizes UJ-X" tagging is applied inconsistently across FRs — present on FR-1, FR-2, FR-5, FR-8, FR-10, FR-11, absent on FR-3, FR-4, FR-6, FR-7, FR-9, FR-12. This matters most for FR-6/FR-7: UJ-1's own "Edge case" beat explicitly depends on the area-expansion-then-empty-state behavior they define, yet neither FR carries a back-reference. Architecture/epics consumers relying on grep-for-"Realizes" to build a traceability matrix will silently miss these. *Fix:* Either tag every FR with "Realizes UJ-X" (or "Enables — no direct UJ" for pure infra FRs like FR-3/FR-12) so the convention is total rather than partial.

## Shape fit — strong

For a Lahore-only internal coordination tool with real but moderate stakes, four UJs and twelve FRs is the right density — enough to cover registration, eligibility, search, notification, and self-service without inflating personas into a market-research exercise the stakeholder never asked for. No RACI matrix, no elaborate demographic segmentation, no multi-region variants — appropriately unformalized given the product's actual scope and the terse-stakeholder drafting reality documented in the addendum.
